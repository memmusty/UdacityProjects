Introduction¶

This Medical Appointment NO-SHOWS dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.

1. ‘No-Show’ tells us if the patient show up their appointment or not. It says ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up
2. ‘ScheduledDay’ tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.
3. ‘Neighborhood’ indicates the location of the hospital.
4. ‘Scholarship’ indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família.
5. ‘SMS_received’ indicates whether 1 or more messages is sent to the patient

This dataset will be investigated to answer the following research questions:

1. What proportion of patients showed up for their appointments, and what proportion  did not?
2. What is the age distribution of the patients
3. What proportion of the patients received SMS?
4. Do people that receive SMS show up for their appointments?
5. What proportion of the patients are on Scholarship?
6. Do patients with scholarship show up for their appointments?

Conclusions¶

Results: From the analysis, it is observed that:

  1. Approximately 80% of the patients showed up for thier appointments
  2. Most of the patients are 30-60 years old.
  3. Less than a 1/3 of the patients received SMS, and more than 2/3 of the patients that received SMS came for their appointments.
  4. Approximately 10% of the patients are on Scholarship, and more than 2/3 of the patients on scholarship showed up for their appointments.

Limitations

  1. Most of the variables are categorical, hence, I could not check for the correlations of the variables to the output to determine which of the has higher influence on the output
  2. Only descriptive statistics was carried out in attempting to answer questions 4 and 6, as a result of the categorical nature of the data.
  3. Even though "Scholarship" and "SMS_Received" may seem like a reasonable influence on whether a patient shows up for a medical appointment or not, and even though a higher proportion of those with scholarship and those that received SMS showed up for their apointments, , it cannot be concluded from the analysis made if Scholarship or SMS increaases the possibility of a patient showing up for thier appointments.
